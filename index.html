Welcome to ME

This is the web presence of Jason Paul Cole, and although I'm not a huge fan of labels I also understand and appreciate their purpose in allowing us to describe ourselves and our interests to one another

My top interests

Skills I use to pay the bills

I write codes and build web sites and web applications. As a front-end web developer I am skilled in the following programming languages (plus their libraries/frameworks listed), and have worked with them in a production environment in a professional setting.

  • HTML 4 and 5, plus a brief side-trek into XHTML.
  • CSS 2 and 3, with pre-processor experience - from most to least - SASS/LESS/Stylus. With SASS I've used the Compass/Bourbon/Neat libraries.
  • Javascript Not having jQuery available is not an issue, pure JS I know well. I am comfortable using jQuery, and have a solid understanding of design patterns. My MVC experience extends to Backbone and Underscore, with Handlebars for templating.
  • CMS
  • Blog frameworks
  • Versioning
  • Development environments I've done the majority of my work on teams in a LAMP stack, with Java/Tomcat coming in a close second. I've also spent time in a couple Ruby on Rails teams, and once worked with the Unity Game System.

  • Known Gallery
    Los Angeles based art gallery focused on street art and fine art projects by local grafitti artists and crews.
  • The Seventh Letter
    Artist collective featuring grafitti artists considered to br the upper echelon of the Los Angeles graf scene - MSK crew reps heavily.     Saber - AWR MSK - Legendary LA graf king best known for his game-changing top-to-bottom piece in the LA River.
  • KVR Audio
    Online community and site supplying current news about VST, AU, RTAS, DX and DSSI/LADSPA plug-ins, iOS and Android App users.     Bitwig Studio - Multi-platform music-creation system for production, remixing and performance with a focus on flexible editing tools and a super-fast workflow.
  • Martsman
    Personal web presence of Martsman, German drumstep production engineer and breakbeat audio string theorist/mentalist. First drop site for new tunes to rinse, influences and old-school drum n' bass classics, thoughts and experiments from his grey matter.
  • Just Born Candy Co.
    Pennsylvania based candy company responsible for the greatest candy line on earth at this time in history, the Mike and Ike, bullet-shaped with a crunchy polished outside which gives way to a soft chewy inner zone. With a plethora of flavors in a solid lineup of available combinations, the personal standout is the Jolly Joes (solo grape), standing a clear leader. Just Born also makes marshmallow Peeps, the vile confection. Heaven and Hell created out of the same machine in a good vs. evil, light battles dark in a coating of sugar.
  • Eloquent Javascript by Marijn Haverbeke
    Javascript book for the developer who has conquered the fundamentals and is looking for the next step in JS domination. Creator of Code Mirror, an in-browser code editor, runs through a quick review of important fundamentals with a focus on keeping your code clean. There are chapters on the DOM, a high-level overview of Object Oriented Programming, code modularity, and HTTP requests. This, in addition to Javascript: The Good Parts, Secrets of the JavaScript Ninja, and O'Reily's Javascript the Definitive Guide - round out the 4 must-read books for the Intermediate Javascript Developer.     Learning JavaScript Design Patterns by Addy Osmanti
  • Bob Jaroc on Vimeo
    Experimental video artist's film dump featuring most of his best released work, ready to stream. His work with Plaid is a personal favorite, but he's also done video for musicians such as Fatboy Slim and OK Go. His video work is non-traditional at the very least, using a variety of material and technique to obtain illusive, abnormal and visually dazzling visual effects that mesh with the neo-electro polyrhythmic audio conjured up by Andy Turner and Ed Handley of duo Plaid.
  • Synthesizers.com
    An online shrine to the graceful and elegantly aged analog synthesizer loved, revered, and worshipped by musicians of all genre, from funk organ thrusters to robot bleep speaking IDM children. Tons of photos revealing beautiful obsession.
  • Loom of Ruin
    Sam McPheeters, of hardcore legend Born Against and experimental post-modern Men's Recovery Project, with a stint in Wrangler Brutes rounding out his musical pedigree. A more than competent visual artist with a clear and developed style, he designs print materials for all the bands on Vermiform, the now-defunct record label he started and ran. The Locust, Towel, Sinking Body, and other inspired post-hardcore outfits made up the VMFM stable. Loom of Ruin, in this incarnation, is the personal blog of McPheeters. News, opinion, unearthed gems from his past magazine columns, and widely agreed as the icing on this cake of a web property - the articles. Hilarious in a way unmatched, living in a world impossible yet familiar, dense with detail.     Loom of Ruin - First 3 Chapters
